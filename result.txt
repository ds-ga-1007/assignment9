The general trend of GDP per Capital in Europe, North America and Oceania are relatively stable.
This is due to the above three continents are mainly developed countries. However, we still see
some decline in those countries in 2008 because of the financial crisis. Another stable-economy
continent is Africa, because it is full of the most under-developed countries. Economy is not
improving. Asia and South America have economic improvement during this period. We can notice
an interesting distribution in Asia, the median is well below the average, which is caused by
the most developed countries in Asia, such as Japan and Singopore, are much richer than the
median economy countries. So the average is much skewed upward.
